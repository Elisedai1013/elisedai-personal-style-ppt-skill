# Slide archetypes

Use these as narrative and compositional starting points, not rigid templates.

## 1. Cover

- Field: Signal Blue, black, or white according to the opening mood.
- Content: one title, optional short subtitle, optional integrated visual.
- Visual form: typography only, full-field sketch, or one metaphor scene.
- Keep at least half the page quiet.

## 2. Typographic question

- Field: pure white.
- Content: one question or up to three short numbered questions.
- Use spacing and scale instead of cards or labels.
- Do not add an illustration unless it changes how the question is understood.

## 3. Decisive thesis

- Field: dark or Signal Blue.
- Content: one spoken statement and at most one support line.
- Visual form: typography only or one sparse white/gray metaphor.
- Avoid decorative blue keywords.

## 4. Metaphor scene

- Field: pure white, dark, or a dominant organic mass.
- Layout: one large scene and one concise statement; either may lead.
- Use scale contrast, tension, direction, or a threshold.
- Blue marks one path, light, active object, change, or result.

## 5. Geometric or spotlight concept

- Field: pure white with a black/blue stage, or dark with a white aperture.
- Use one window, circle, grid, boundary, or organic shape to organize the idea.
- Keep symbols sparse and largely unlabeled.
- Use for known/unknown, search, territory, capability, or transformation.

## 6. Full-field chapter break

- Field: Signal Blue or black.
- Content: one short section statement.
- Visual form: one small white/gray sketch or gesture integrated into the field.
- Do not add an agenda inventory.

## 7. Case, person, or quote

- Field: pure white with treated evidence.
- Layout: 38–45% text / 55–62% image, or an editorial overlap.
- Treat the photo background with grayscale, pale blue, or duotone.
- Keep the quote or principle to one sentence.

## 8. Process or mechanism

- Field: pure white.
- Use one black/gray structure and one blue path through 3–5 moments.
- Prefer a continuous visual journey over separate icons.
- Keep labels short and subordinate.

## 9. Comparison

- Field: pure white.
- Use two flat regions separated by whitespace, a hairline, or one central visual boundary.
- Use blue only for the changed or recommended element.
- Avoid two rounded cards.

## 10. Data or evidence

- Field: pure white; pale blue may exist only inside the treated image.
- Use black/gray context and one blue claim-bearing series or number.
- Crop screenshots to the evidence-bearing region and normalize irrelevant colors.
- State the meaning in the title; do not add several explanatory callouts.

## 11. Closing

- Field: Signal Blue for a decisive ending; dark or pure white for a reflective synthesis.
- Resolve the opening question with one implication or action.
- Visual form may echo an earlier metaphor, but do not reuse the same image asset.
- Do not end with a generic “Thank you.”

## Selection test

Before choosing an archetype, ask what the visual must contribute:

- Nothing beyond emphasis → typography only.
- A felt analogy → metaphor scene.
- A boundary or invisible mechanism → geometric/spotlight stage.
- A pause or chapter shift → full-field sketch.
- Proof → treated evidence.
